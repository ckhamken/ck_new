Project Overview

High-speed automated press bending cell with blank feeder, transfer grippers, and tool die design. During the bending process, the product is reoriented at an intermediate position between presses by a flipper module. Version 1.0 was a pre-existing proven design but had significant moment of inertia from the amount of hardware used to secure the product during rotation. Version 2.0 started from scratch with just the required motions — clamp and rotate — and worked to achieve them in the most simplified way possible. The result was lighter in every regard: hardware count, rotational moment of inertia, footprint, and maintenance. The quicker flipper motion also meant transfer arms no longer waited for the flip to complete before proceeding, enabling a velocity increase and improving overall throughput.

Flipper Version
From v1.0 to v2.0 — same motions, radically simpler

The v2.0 flipper redesign started by stripping the problem back to the two required motions: clamp and rotate. Every component that didn't directly serve those motions was eliminated. The result reduced rotational moment of inertia, footprint, hardware count, and maintenance requirements — and freed the transfer arms to run at higher velocity since the flip now completes before the press cycle does.
