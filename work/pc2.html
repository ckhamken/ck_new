Project Overview

A multidisciplinary senior capstone spanning three semesters between 9 mechanical, 7 electrical, and 2 software engineering undergraduates at Auburn University. The goal: design, build, and operate a fully autonomous lawnmower capable of navigating a grass field, avoiding static obstacles (flower beds) and dynamic obstacles (an RC-controlled dog), and mowing as much area as possible within the time limit. The completed machine — nicknamed "Moe" — was entered into the 7th Annual ION Robotic Lawnmower Competition in Beavercreek, Ohio, placing 3rd overall. Competition scoring was 80% mowing performance and 20% technical documentation and presentation.
